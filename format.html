Competition Format & Rules

Round 1 – School Paper

- 20 multiple-choice questions - 3 points for each correct answer, -1 for each wrong answer - 5 options per question (A–E) - No calculators or technology allowed - Paper lasts 1 hour

Qualification

- Top 10–20 students qualify for the national finals - Ties are first broken by the number of correct answers - If still tied, the mean of the hardest questions answered is used - If a tie persists, both students qualify

National Final

- Round-robin stage first to determine the top 8 - Knockout stage follows to determine the winner - Final lasts approximately 2–3 hours - No calculators or technology allowed - Solving on whiteboards is encouraged

Prizes & Certificates

- Top 33% of Round 1 receive a certificate of commendation - All finalists receive a certificate of qualification - Top 3 winners receive medals (gold, silver, bronze) - Top 4 finalists receive a book - Everyone receives a participation certificate - Best-in-school certificate awarded per school

Content

- All questions are based on A-level maths integration - Problems encourage creative thinking and logical reasoning
